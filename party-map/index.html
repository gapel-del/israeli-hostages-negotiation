Al Jazeera Effect

Built with gas-field revenue, the pan-Arab network created Qatar's maverick global image — and repeatedly drew backlash:

  • Critically covered Iraq War and Arab Spring — infuriating regional autocrats
  • Supported Muslim Brotherhood narratives — flashpoint with Egypt and Saudi Arabia
  • Bush reportedly contemplated bombing its Doha HQ during Iraq War (White House denied)
2017 Gulf Crisis

Saudi Arabia, UAE, Bahrain, and Egypt severed ties and blockaded Qatar — with a green light from the Trump administration. A ground invasion was considered.

Triggers: Al Jazeera coverage · Muslim Brotherhood ties · relationship with Iran · hosting Hamas leadership
Outcome: Blockade collapsed after 3 years with zero concessions. Qatar doubled down on mediation.

    Gaza deaths — Gaza Ministry of Health daily bulletins, cross-referenced with UN OCHA situation reports. The MoH tracks documented deaths at health facilities; actual toll is likely higher due to bodies under rubble. OCHA oPt Casualties

    Building destruction — UNOSAT satellite damage assessments, published Jan 2024, Sep 2024, and Mar 2025. Numbers represent structures assessed as destroyed or severely/moderately damaged in Gaza Strip. UNOSAT 2024 · UNOSAT Mar 2025

    Hamas approval — Palestinian Center for Policy and Survey Research (PCPSR) polls conducted in the West Bank and Gaza. Question: "Hamas's decision to launch the Oct 7 attack was correct." Figures reflect Gaza respondents where available. PCPSR
